# YouTube Shorts Monetization Compliance Checklist

> **Purpose:** Reference document for the YouTube Shorts automation system.
> Every generated Short MUST pass ALL sections before publishing.
> **Last updated:** March 2026

---

## 1. YPP Eligibility Requirements

### Tier 1 — Fan Funding (Entry Level)
- 500 subscribers
- 3 million valid public Shorts views in the last 90 days (OR 3,000 public watch hours on long-form in 12 months)
- 3 public posts in the last 90 days
- Access to: Super Thanks, Super Chat, Super Stickers, Channel Memberships

### Tier 2 — Full Ad Revenue
- 1,000 subscribers
- 10 million valid public Shorts views in the last 90 days (OR 4,000 public watch hours on long-form in 12 months)
- Must accept the **Shorts Monetization Module**
- Creator keeps **45%** of allocated revenue (YouTube keeps 55%)

### General YPP Prerequisites
- [ ] No active Community Guideline strikes
- [ ] Two-step verification enabled on Google account
- [ ] Advanced features enabled
- [ ] Active AdSense account linked
- [ ] Channel complies with all YouTube monetization policies
- [ ] Located in an eligible country

---

## 2. Revenue Model — How Shorts Pay

- Ad revenue is pooled from ads shown **between videos** in the Shorts Feed
- Revenue is allocated into the **Creator Pool** based on **engaged views**
- Only "engaged views" count — legitimate views from real users that meet YouTube's policies
- If a Short uses music from YouTube's library, revenue is split between the creator and music rights holders
- Shorts **without music** allocate 100% of associated revenue to the Creator Pool
- Expected RPM: **$0.03–$0.10 per 1,000 views** (varies by niche and region)

### What Makes Views Ineligible
- Non-original Shorts (unedited clips, reuploads, compilations with nothing added)
- Artificial/fake views (bots, automated scrolling)
- Views on Shorts inconsistent with advertiser-friendly guidelines
- Shorts over 1 minute containing claimed content are **blocked entirely**

## 3. CRITICAL — Inauthentic Content Policy (Updated July 2025)

> **This is the #1 risk for an automated channel.** In January 2026, YouTube terminated 16 channels with a combined 4.7 billion views and 35 million subscribers in a single enforcement wave targeting "AI slop."

### What Gets You Killed

- ❌ Content created entirely by bots/scripts/software with **minimal human input**
- ❌ Auto-generated slideshows, text-to-speech videos, or AI content without significant editing
- ❌ Highly similar or identical videos uploaded in bulk
- ❌ Compilations with no added value (no commentary, no context, no creative edits)
- ❌ Low-effort edits of others' content (basic filter, speed change, crop)
- ❌ Reused/recycled content without transformative effort
- ❌ Content produced using a template and repeated at scale with minimal variation
- ❌ Image slideshows or scrolling text with minimal/no narrative
- ❌ Similar repetitive content with low educational value or minimal variation

### What Keeps You Safe

- ✅ Each video has **substantively different content** from others on the channel
- ✅ Original scripts with unique perspectives, insights, or analysis
- ✅ Human voiceover (or voice that demonstrates genuine creative direction)
- ✅ Significant visual editing with effects, transitions, and original graphics
- ✅ Clear narrative structure (hook → content → conclusion)
- ✅ Creator's voice/personality comes through in each video
- ✅ Educational or entertainment value clearly present
- ✅ Varied topics, angles, or subjects — not just the same template with swapped data

### Enforcement Consequences
1. **Demonetization** — Channel loses YPP status. Can reapply after 30 days (90 days for severe violations)
2. **Channel Suspension** — Content hidden, 7–90 day suspension
3. **Full Termination** — Permanent. All content deleted. No recovery.

> ⚠️ This policy applies **channel-wide**. A few bad videos can take down your entire channel's monetization.

## 4. Reused Content Policy (Separate from Inauthentic)

### Not Allowed
- Exclusively reading text from websites or news feeds you didn't create
- Clips from shows edited together with little/no narrative
- Short videos compiled from other social media
- Collections of songs from different artists
- Non-verbal reactions to videos without added voice commentary
- Taking someone else's content with minimal changes

### Allowed
- Reused content where the creator is visible or explains how they added to the content
- Edited footage with audio and visual effects showing substantive editing
- Commentary, review, or reaction with genuine voice-over analysis
- Remixed content with original additions on Shorts

## 5. Advertiser-Friendly Content Guidelines

> All Shorts must follow these to earn ad revenue. Violations result in "limited or no ads" (yellow icon) or "no ads" (red icon).

### Topics That Risk Demonetization (Yellow/Red Icon)

| Topic | Green (Full Ads) | Yellow (Limited) | Red (No Ads) |
|-------|-----------------|------------------|--------------|
| **Language** | Mild profanity OK | Moderate profanity ("shit") | Extreme profanity/slurs in title or thumbnail |
| **Violence** | Unedited gameplay OK | War, tragedy, conflicts discussed | Graphic violence as focal point |
| **Adult content** | Educational (textbook level) | Suggestive themes | Sexually explicit, pornographic |
| **Drugs** | — | References to soft drugs | Promotion of hard drug use |
| **Controversial issues** | Non-graphic discussion | Detailed/dramatic coverage | Graphic/sensationalized content |
| **Harmful acts** | — | Pranks, stunts, challenges | Suicide/death-related pranks |
| **Firearms** | — | Proper use, sport shooting | Promoting illegal use |
| **Hateful content** | — | — | Any hate speech or discrimination |
| **Misinformation** | — | — | False claims about elections, dangerous medical advice |

### Content That Is Always Ineligible
- Sexually explicit or gratifying content
- Content involving child-friendly characters in violent/sexual/distasteful acts
- Deepfakes of real people without disclosure
- Content promoting harm to oneself or others
- False claims undermining trust in elections or democratic processes

### January 2026 Update
- Non-graphic or dramatized coverage of self-harm, abuse, suicide, domestic abuse, abortion, sexual harassment is now eligible for **full** monetization
- Child abuse and eating disorders content **remains restricted**

## 6. Copyright Compliance

### Rules for Automated Content
- [ ] No copyrighted music unless using YouTube's Audio Library or licensed tracks
- [ ] No unedited clips from movies, TV shows, or other creators
- [ ] No copyrighted images without license/permission
- [ ] All visual assets must be original, licensed, or properly attributed
- [ ] If using music: be aware it splits your Creator Pool revenue with rights holders
- [ ] Shorts over 1 minute with claimed content are **completely blocked** from monetization

### Content ID System
- YouTube automatically scans all uploads against its Content ID database
- Copyright holders can: claim revenue, block your video, or issue a takedown
- 3 copyright strikes = **channel termination**

## 7. Automation-Specific Safeguards

> These are the rules we derive from all the above, specifically for our automation system.

### MUST HAVE in Every Generated Short
- [ ] **Original script** — not scraped or minimally rewritten from a single source
- [ ] **Unique narrative voice** — each video feels distinct, not templated
- [ ] **Human-quality voiceover** — if using TTS, it must be high-quality and sound natural; ideally use a real human voice or heavily post-processed AI voice
- [ ] **Substantive visual editing** — not just static images or basic slideshows
- [ ] **Varied structure** — don't use the exact same template for every video
- [ ] **Clear value proposition** — educational, entertaining, or informative
- [ ] **No copyrighted material** — all assets original or licensed
- [ ] **Duration ≤ 60 seconds** if using any third-party audio (to avoid claimed content block)

### Content Variation Strategy
- Rotate visual styles across videos
- Vary hook formats (question, statement, shock, story)
- Mix content subtypes within the niche
- Change pacing, transitions, and effects between videos
- Use different background music/sound design per video

### Red Flags Our System Must Detect Before Publishing
- [ ] Script similarity > 70% with any previous video → BLOCK
- [ ] Same visual template used > 3 times consecutively → WARN
- [ ] No voiceover or narrative present → BLOCK
- [ ] Contains potential copyrighted material → BLOCK
- [ ] Touches advertiser-unfriendly topics without safe framing → REVIEW
- [ ] Bulk upload pattern (> 5 videos in 24 hours with similar structure) → THROTTLE

## 8. Quality Principles Summary

YouTube has repeatedly stated they reward channels that feel **"sustainable, not just viral."** The algorithm and manual reviewers look for:

1. **Authenticity** — Does this feel like a real creator made it with intention?
2. **Originality** — Is the substance different from video to video?
3. **Value** — Would a viewer feel their time was well spent?
4. **Effort** — Is there clear evidence of creative work (writing, editing, production)?
5. **Consistency** — Does the channel maintain quality across uploads?

---

## 9. Pre-Publish Checklist (Per Video)

```
□ Script is original and not duplicated from previous videos
□ Voiceover is present and adds genuine narrative value
□ Visuals are original/licensed and substantively edited
□ No copyrighted music, footage, or images
□ Video structure differs meaningfully from last 5 uploads
□ Content is advertiser-friendly (no yellow/red triggers)
□ Duration is appropriate (under 60s if any third-party audio)
□ Title and thumbnail are not clickbait or misleading
□ Description and tags are accurate and non-spammy
□ Self-certification questionnaire answers are honest
```
