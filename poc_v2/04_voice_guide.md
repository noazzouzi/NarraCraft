# Voice Guide — ElevenLabs

## Voice Setup

**Character:** Jill Valentine
**Voice style:** Young woman, late 20s. Confident, military-trained, but human.
Think "soldier telling a campfire story about something that still haunts her."

**ElevenLabs settings:**
- Pick a female voice: confident, clear, mid-range pitch, slight edge
- Recommended voices to try: "Rachel", "Bella", or browse for a match
- Stability: ~0.45 (expressive, not robotic)
- Similarity boost: ~0.75
- Style exaggeration: ~0.30

---

## Scene-by-Scene Voice Lines

### Scene 1 (5 sec) — Hook
**Direction:** Punchy, suspicious. "Never really one of us" should sting.
```
Did you know? Albert Wesker — our captain — was never really one of us.
```

### Scene 2 (6 sec) — Angry
**Direction:** Controlled anger. "Kidnapped" hits hard. Building tension.
```
He was kidnapped as a child. Taken by Umbrella's founder, Oswell Spencer.
```

### Scene 3 (6 sec) — Horror
**Direction:** Slower, more somber. "Dozens of children" — let it land. Horrified.
```
Spencer called it Project W. Dozens of children, locked in labs, injected with experimental viruses.
```

### Scene 4 (5 sec) — Disgust
**Direction:** Spat out with contempt. Short, punchy. "Perfect human weapon" — each word deliberate.
```
A eugenics program. To build the perfect human weapon.
```

### Scene 5 (6 sec) — Weight
**Direction:** Quieter. "Only one survived" — pause before "Wesker." Awe mixed with dread.
```
Out of all those children, only one survived. Wesker.
```

### Scene 6 (6 sec) — Building
**Direction:** Gaining momentum through the list. "Programmed" is the gut punch at the end.
```
His speed, his strength, his obsession with evolution — none of it was natural. It was all programmed.
```

### Scene 7 (6 sec) — Resignation
**Direction:** Tired, heavy. She's been fighting this man for years, and now she knows the truth.
```
The man Chris and I spent years fighting was Spencer's weapon from the very start.
```

### Scene 8 (6 sec) — The Reveal
**Direction:** Quiet conviction → hardens on the last line. "Built to be one" is the mic drop.
Slight pause before the final sentence.
```
Wesker never chose to be a monster. He was built to be one.
```

---

## Tips
1. Generate each scene as a **separate audio clip** — easier to time in CapCut
2. If pacing feels off, regenerate just that scene
3. Total audio: ~46 seconds (leaving room for transitions)
4. Save as: `voice_01.mp3` through `voice_08.mp3`
5. For the POC test, you can use ElevenLabs free tier or even a free TTS to validate the workflow before paying
