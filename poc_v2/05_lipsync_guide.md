# Lip-Sync Guide — Narrator Scenes Only

## Which Scenes Need Lip-Sync?

Only the narrator close-up scenes (where Jill faces camera and speaks):
- Scene 2, Scene 4, Scene 6, Scene 8

The other scenes (1, 3, 5, 7) are static images with voiceover — handled
entirely in CapCut with Ken Burns camera movement. No lip-sync needed.

---

## Tool Options (ranked by ease for image + audio → talking video)

### Option A: Hedra (hedra.com) — Simplest
1. Go to hedra.com → sign up
2. Click "Create"
3. Upload: Jill close-up scene image (e.g., scene_02.png)
4. Upload: matching voice clip (e.g., voice_02.mp3)
5. Generate → download
6. Repeat for scenes 4, 6, 8

**Pros:** One step, purpose-built for this
**Cons:** Free tier has watermark, works best with face-only close-ups

### Option B: Kling AI Two-Step — Higher quality but costs more
1. Go to klingai.com → Image-to-Video
2. Upload scene image → prompt: "woman speaking to camera, subtle natural
   head movement, slight expressions" → generate 5-6 second video
3. Go to Lip Sync → upload that video + voice clip → generate
4. Download result

**Pros:** More natural motion (head tilts, micro-expressions)
**Cons:** Two steps, uses more credits

### Option C: D-ID (d-id.com) — Reliable fallback
1. Go to d-id.com → Creative Reality Studio
2. Upload image + audio
3. Generate → download

**Pros:** Reliable, well-established
**Cons:** Trial credits limited

---

## Recommended Approach

1. **Test all three** with Scene 2 (same image + same audio)
2. Compare quality side by side
3. Pick the winner for the remaining 3 scenes
4. If free tiers run out: mix tools (e.g., Hedra for 2 scenes, D-ID for 2 scenes)

---

## What Good Lip-Sync Looks Like

- Mouth opens and closes in sync with the audio syllables
- Jaw moves naturally (not just lips flapping)
- Subtle head movement (slight tilts, nods) — not frozen stiff
- Eyes stay natural (blink occasionally, maintain direction)
- No face warping or distortion
- Expression feels consistent with the source image

## What to Watch For (red flags)

- Mouth moving but not matching the actual words
- Face "melting" or losing shape
- Eyes going dead/glassy
- Teeth appearing/disappearing unnaturally
- Sudden jumps in head position
- Background warping around the face
